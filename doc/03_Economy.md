# Frontier 10052 — Economy

## Model

Markets are driven by production, consumption, storage, transport capacity, disruption, policy, and information delay. Locations maintain inventories, target reserves, inbound expectations, and confidence levels. Prices respond to scarcity, urgency, legality, spoilage, storage constraints, and known traffic.

## Information delay

A displayed price is an observation, not universal truth. Each quote records location, timestamp, source, confidence, and expected demand drivers. Remote prices age while ships travel. Players may profit by arriving before competitors or lose money when old reports become obsolete.

## Money flows

Income includes trade margins, contracts, passengers, salvage, information brokerage, services, favors converted into value, and occasional speculation.

Persistent costs include:

- fuel and metric-drive consumables;
- wages, food, medical care, and passenger support;
- docking, handling, permits, taxes, and Gate charges;
- insurance, debt service, fines, bribes, and legal representation;
- maintenance, spare parts, ammunition, repairs, and refits.

## Credit and insurance

Credit depends on commercial trust, collateral, jurisdiction, faction standing, ship condition, and recent performance. Insurance prices reflect route risk, cargo legality, captain history, crew qualifications, and evidence quality. Claims can trigger investigations.

## Dynamic events

Strikes, accidents, epidemics, blockades, piracy, migration surges, Gate recalibration, fleet movements, discoveries, and false rumors change production and demand. Effects propagate at ship speed.

## Balance principles

No permanent risk-free route. Large profit should require capital, knowledge, timing, relationships, or danger. Wealth expands options but does not eliminate maintenance, law, social obligations, or the standing cost of Gate travel.
