# Frontier 10052 — Star Systems

## Astronomical grounding

Use real stars and plausible scale wherever practical. Fictional worlds, stations, habitats, settlements, and local histories can be layered around known systems without treating astronomy as decoration.

## Canonical regions

- Sol Heritage Zone;
- Core Belt;
- Compact Space;
- Lyra Reach;
- Meridian Worlds;
- Old Battlegrounds;
- Freehold Rim;
- Silent Margins;
- Threshold;
- Andromedan destinations beyond the Bridge.

## Opening region

The game begins in Sol. Earth and its heritage habitats are ceremonial, beautiful, regulated, and economically stagnant. Mars is industrial, wealthy, politically connected, and essential to shipbuilding. Pluto Gateway handles migration, freight consolidation, unofficial trade, and outward routes. Belt installations provide fuel, repair, salvage, labor, and old spacer culture.

## System specification

Every meaningful system requires:

- astronomical identity and coordinates;
- bodies, stations, and travel geometry;
- jurisdiction and faction influence;
- production, consumption, storage, and trade role;
- hazards, repair access, and law enforcement;
- visual, audio, language, and cultural identity;
- signature characters, contracts, mysteries, and events;
- information sources and communication latency.

## Scale strategy

The long-term map may contain about 500 meaningful stars, but production begins with a small, dense cluster. Expansion uses tiers: fully authored hubs, authored secondary systems, systemic frontier systems, and sparse transit or exploration sites.

## Discovery

The map records what the player has reason to know. Routes, hazards, prices, laws, and settlements may be uncertain, outdated, disputed, or deliberately concealed.
