# Frontier 10052 — Trading

## Cargo model

Cargo categories include food, water, medicine, industrial feedstock, machine parts, high-value components, luxury goods, cultural artifacts, biological material, weapons, controlled substances, data archives, passengers, and migration manifests.

Every cargo record includes mass, volume, handling class, legality by jurisdiction, storage requirements, degradation, provenance, insurance class, purchase evidence, and market tags.

## Market interface

The player sees current local inventory and historical or imported remote reports. A quote must show price age, source, confidence, legality, expected demand, available volume, handling constraints, and likely competition.

## Contracts

Trade contracts define issuer, beneficiary, quantity, quality, deadline, delivery evidence, jurisdiction, payment, penalties, inspection terms, and hidden complications. Partial delivery, delay, substitution, and renegotiation are supported where appropriate.

## Negotiation

Prices and terms may change through reputation, information, urgency, crew expertise, bundled services, credit, favors, or threats. Negotiation is bounded by the counterparty’s incentives and authority.

## Information brokerage

Information is first-class inventory. Market reports, strike notices, warrants, migration rules, salvage coordinates, medical alerts, Gate queues, military movements, and corroborating evidence can be copied, sold, withheld, leaked, poisoned, or used privately.

## Illegal trade

Smuggling requires concealment, forged paperwork, route timing, contacts, acceptable crew, and a plan for inspection or betrayal. Illegal cargo offers high margins but affects law, insurance, crew trust, criminal credibility, and public notoriety.

## Anti-exploit rules

Markets react to player volume. Repeated profitable routes attract competitors, scrutiny, shortages of transport capacity, or policy changes. Save/reload must not reveal perfectly deterministic inspection outcomes.
