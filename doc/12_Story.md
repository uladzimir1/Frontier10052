# Frontier 10052 — Story

## Premise

The player gains control of an aging merchant ship through inheritance, purchase under pressure, or debt. The vessel carries legal obligations, hidden history, and a crew whose loyalty is not guaranteed.

## Main structure

1. **Inheritance or debt:** establish ship, crew, financial pressure, and Sol.
2. **The first run:** reveal that delayed information may be more valuable than cargo.
3. **The frontier opens:** factions compete for the player’s service and trust.
4. **The Andromeda question:** evidence challenges both human-space and Federation narratives.
5. **The crossing:** choose whether, how, with whom, and under whose sponsorship to enter Andromeda.
6. **The cost of paradise:** experience a functioning society with real hospitality, boundaries, and contradictions.
7. **Stay, serve, expose, profit, reform, or escape:** ending emerges from accumulated history.

## Themes

Distance, trust, decline, migration, institutional memory, debt, home, freedom, maintenance, and the moral cost of reliable systems.

## Agency

The main story provides pressure and irreversible choices without replacing the sandbox. Critical outcomes depend on relationships, evidence, promises, debts, crimes, faction standing, crew condition, and prior behavior—not a single final dialogue option.

## Delivery

Narrative appears through contracts, delayed messages, crew conversations, manifests, invoices, station design, scars, recordings, legal proceedings, rumors, and environmental evidence. The codex records claims with sources and uncertainty rather than presenting omniscient truth.

## End states

Endings can include permanent settlement, public service, commercial power, exposure of wrongdoing, criminal escape, attempted reform, return to human space, crew dissolution, or continued wandering. No ending should erase the consequences of the journey.
