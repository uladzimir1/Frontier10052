# Frontier 10052 — Vision

## Product statement

**Frontier 10052** is a cinematic hard-science-fiction trading RPG and civilization simulation set in 10052 AD. The player commands an aging merchant ship at the edge of a technologically extraordinary but unevenly declining human civilization.

Earth is culturally sacred and economically diminished. Mars and Pluto are practical centers of industry and migration. Human space is fragmented among institutions, corporations, freeholds, syndicates, faiths, and criminal networks. Andromeda offers a regulated democratic alternative that is easier to enter than to leave.

## Player fantasy

The player is a captain, trader, investigator, employer, debtor, witness, smuggler, rescuer, and carrier of news. The ship is home, tool, liability, and character. The crew are people with beliefs, memories, loyalties, needs, and boundaries.

## Creative pillars

1. **Distance has weight.** There is no faster-than-light communication. News, prices, warrants, and messages move aboard ships.
2. **The ship remembers.** Wear, repairs, modifications, and crew rituals persist.
3. **Trust is playable.** Information has age, provenance, confidence, and incentives.
4. **Civilization declines unevenly.** Beauty, competence, kindness, corruption, scarcity, and fatigue coexist.
5. **Every solution has a social consequence.** Trade, law, favors, stealth, bribery, rescue, flight, and combat all create obligations.

## Product boundaries

The game is single-player first, with future multiplayer readiness. Progression is primarily access, reliability, relationships, knowledge, and specialization—not immunity from economics, maintenance, law, or scarcity.

## Success criterion

Players should tell stories such as: “I reached the station before the warrant,” “the warning was worth more than the cargo,” and “Andromeda was better than home, but I still had to escape.”
