# Frontier 10052 — Artificial Intelligence

## In-world canon

Artificial intelligence is legally and socially contested, not casually omnipotent. Jurisdictions permit different combinations of narrow expert systems, supervised cognition, embedded ship assistance, autonomous agents, and restricted “second minds.” AI has compute, energy, maintenance, data, legal, and institutional costs.

AI cannot ignore physical communication delay, obtain universal truth, replace scarce sensors, or safely control every institution. Records may be biased, incomplete, manipulated, or legally sealed.

## Game AI architecture

Use deterministic or reproducible systems where practical:

- utility scoring for needs and priorities;
- behavior trees or planners for tactical activity;
- explicit state machines for contracts and encounters;
- simulation services for economy, factions, law, and information;
- memory records for crew and important NPCs.

## Crew AI

Crew decisions combine skills, assignment, stress, fatigue, beliefs, trust, relationships, legal risk, and remembered events. Explanations must be available to narrative and UI layers.

## Economic and faction AI

Agents react to inventories, reports, confidence, goals, resources, and travel time. They do not use hidden omniscient market truth. Factions move ships, issue contracts, enforce law, and change policy through explicit rules.

## Generative AI policy

External language models may support optional dialogue variation, rumor wording, codex assistance, or developer tools. Canon, quest state, economy, legal outcomes, and rewards remain validated by deterministic game logic. Generated text must use approved context, structured outputs, moderation, caching, cost limits, privacy controls, and graceful offline fallback.

## Testing

Record seeds and decision traces. Validate determinism, bounded behavior, performance, exploit resistance, and understandable reasons for important decisions.
