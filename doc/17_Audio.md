# Frontier 10052 — Audio

## Pillars

Audio communicates physicality, scale, ship condition, regional culture, information, and danger. It should make the aging vessel feel inhabited even during quiet travel.

## Ship soundscape

Pumps, fans, relays, coolant flow, hull stress, cargo mechanisms, airlocks, thrusters, footsteps, tools, alarms, and improvised repairs vary with location, power state, damage, maintenance, and crew activity. Important systems develop recognizable signatures.

## Space and combat

Exterior space remains physically restrained. Cockpit, suit, hull conduction, sensors, radio, and structural vibration communicate action. Weapons, impacts, decompression, damaged engines, and defensive fields prioritize clarity over spectacle.

## Regional identity

Earth heritage spaces sound ceremonial and controlled. Mars is industrial and rhythmic. Pluto mixes freight, migration, machinery, and unofficial radio. Freeholds use adapted equipment and personal soundmarks. Andromeda is clear, calm, coherent, and subtly regulated.

## Music

Adaptive music responds to region, danger, discovery, crew state, story, and travel phase. Silence and machinery are valid musical choices. Themes should transform rather than restart mechanically at every state change.

## Communications

Radio carries law, commerce, rumor, culture, emergency traffic, advertisements, and delayed messages. Signal quality, encryption, distance, source, and ship equipment affect presentation.

## Voice

Prioritize key characters, crew arcs, cinematic moments, warnings, and culturally important ambient speech. Procedural text must remain readable without voice.

## Accessibility and implementation

Provide subtitles, speaker labels, radio transcripts, dynamic-range options, independent buses, tinnitus-safe alternatives, and visual alerts. Use event-driven audio with parameterized states and strict asset budgets.
