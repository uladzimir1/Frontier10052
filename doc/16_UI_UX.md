# Frontier 10052 — UI/UX

## Principles

The interface should feel like a polished shipboard operating system, not a generic admin dashboard. Common actions are fast; expert detail is progressively disclosed. Critical states use labels, shape, hierarchy, motion, and sound rather than color alone.

## Navigation

Major spaces are Bridge, Map, Market, Contracts, Cargo, Ship, Crew, Messages, Journal, Codex, and Settings. Context persists when switching views. Urgent alerts do not destroy the player’s current work.

## Information provenance

Every price, rumor, warrant, political report, and route warning exposes source, timestamp, confidence, corroboration, and geographic scope. The player must understand uncertainty without parsing raw tables.

## Key screens

- HUD and flight instruments;
- galaxy/system map and route planner;
- market comparison and cargo manifest;
- ship schematic, power, heat, wear, and repair;
- crew roster, assignments, relationships, and needs;
- contract terms and evidence;
- messages, news, warrants, and source chains;
- save, accessibility, controls, and diagnostics.

## Interaction

Support mouse, keyboard, controller, and touch where relevant. Provide sensible focus order, remapping, hold/toggle choices, confirmation for destructive actions, undo where safe, and clear disabled-state explanations.

## Accessibility

Text scaling, high contrast, color-vision-safe encoding, subtitles, speaker labels, captioned radio, reduced motion, camera-shake controls, audio visualization, input remapping, difficulty assists, pause support, and screen-reader-compatible website semantics.

## Testing

Test comprehension of price age, route risk, ship dependencies, crew refusal, legal status, and contract terms. Measure errors and hesitation, not only completion time.
