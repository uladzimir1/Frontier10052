# Frontier 10052 — Travel

## Travel layers

1. **Local flight:** stations, orbits, moons, planets, belts, hazards, patrols, docking, and tactical encounters.
2. **Metric-drive travel:** interstellar journeys with time, fuel, pinch decay, reliability, route uncertainty, and information value.
3. **Gate transit:** rare, scheduled, bureaucratic, strategically transformative, and extremely expensive.

## Physical rules

Warp moves ships, never messages. Metric drives cannot operate near strong gravity wells, so conventional departure and arrival phases remain meaningful. Travel time affects crew, ship wear, cargo degradation, deadlines, news value, and warrant propagation.

## Route planning

Inputs include distance, drive rating, consumables, gravity-well exit time, hazards, Silent Zone detours, patrol coverage, repair access, market age, crew fatigue, permits, warrants, and likely courier traffic.

## Gate travel

Builder Gates are operated by the Galactic Gate Authority but cannot be built or fully understood by humanity. Each activation has a large standing cost plus mass-energy charges. Co-manifests, sponsorship, migration programs, debt, and political qualification can make passage possible.

## Encounters

Travel events include breakdowns, distress calls, inspections, piracy, debris, convoy offers, medical incidents, crew conflict, false navigation data, salvage traces, and changing destination priorities.

## UX

The route planner distinguishes facts, estimates, rumors, and unknowns. It shows travel-time ranges, consumables, legal exposure, expected market age on arrival, alternate stops, and consequences of delay.

## MVP

Implement Sol local flight, a gravity-boundary departure, one nearby-star warp route, travel time, consumable use, one event chain, arrival-state changes, and a cinematic but skippable transition.
