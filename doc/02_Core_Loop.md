# Frontier 10052 — Core Loop

## Canonical loop

1. Gather market, political, navigational, legal, and personal intelligence.
2. Select a destination, contract, cargo, passengers, and risk profile.
3. Prepare fuel, pinch, maintenance, crew assignments, permits, cover stories, and route.
4. Undock and travel through local orbital space to the warp boundary.
5. Resolve travel events, crew developments, breakdowns, encounters, and changing priorities.
6. Arrive with information that may be current, stale, unique, false, or dangerous.
7. Trade, deliver, negotiate, investigate, repair, recruit, salvage, or fight.
8. Persist consequences and choose the next run.

## Run quality

Every run should create at least one meaningful change: profit or debt, new knowledge, ship wear, a relationship shift, legal exposure, a contact, a discovered route, or altered regional state.

## Pacing

Preparation should be fast for routine runs and deep for high-risk operations. Travel should alternate quiet shipboard time, decisions, events, and occasional cinematic transitions. Arrival must feel consequential because market conditions and political facts may have changed.

## Long-term loop

Repeated runs build a network of routes, sources, clients, enemies, crew bonds, obligations, and evidence. The player gradually moves from Sol through nearby real stars, frontier regions, Gate termini, and eventually Andromeda.

## Anti-grind rule

Repetition must change context. Markets, relationships, news, law, ship condition, and regional events should prevent identical optimal loops. Profitable routes attract competitors and enforcement; shortages resolve; rumors decay; crew develop opinions.

## Telemetry

Measure time spent preparing, traveling, trading, reading provenance, maintaining the ship, and recovering from failure. Tune for comprehension and meaningful choice—not maximum click frequency.
