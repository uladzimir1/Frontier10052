# Frontier 10052 — Combat

## Goals

Combat is real-time, tactical, dangerous, and consequential. It emphasizes detection, positioning, signatures, subsystem damage, escape windows, surrender, boarding risk, and aftermath rather than frictionless destruction.

## Encounter phases

1. Detection or warning.
2. Identification, credentials, and intent assessment.
3. Maneuver, negotiation, bluff, bribery, or preparation.
4. Engagement, pursuit, boarding, surrender, or escape.
5. Rescue, evidence, repairs, legal response, and retaliation.

## Player options

Avoid detection, reduce signature, transmit credentials, negotiate, bribe, bluff, call a contact, dump cargo, surrender contraband, flee toward patrol coverage, disable pursuit, fight, surrender, board, or resist boarding.

## Systems

Weapons and defenses consume ammunition, power, heat capacity, mass, maintenance, and legal permissions. Damage targets hull, engines, thrusters, radiators, sensors, power, weapons, life support, cargo, and crew spaces.

## Consequences

Victory may still produce injury, decompression, cargo loss, repair debt, insurance denial, evidence, warrants, trauma, faction retaliation, or crew conflict. Disabled enemies create rescue, salvage, prisoner, and legal choices.

## Boarding

Boarding requires matched velocity, access, pressure control, security, medical readiness, and clear objectives. It is high-risk and not a routine loot animation.

## AI

Opponents value survival, objectives, law, relationships, and available information. Pirates prefer profitable surrender; patrols seek compliance and evidence; mercenaries follow contracts; desperate actors make mistakes.

## MVP

One pursuit encounter supporting avoidance, inspection, bribery, cargo sacrifice, subsystem disabling, escape, and surrender. Destruction should be only one possible conclusion.
