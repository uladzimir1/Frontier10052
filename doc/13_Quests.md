# Frontier 10052 — Quests and Contracts

## Types

Freight, courier, passenger, migration, salvage, rescue, investigation, escort, repair, negotiation, debt, smuggling, bounty, legal defense, crew loyalty, exploration, and faction operations.

## Contract schema

A contract defines issuer, beneficiary, objective, cargo or target, deadline, jurisdiction, required evidence, payment, expenses, penalties, legal terms, secrets, allowed substitutions, failure transformations, and callback hooks.

## Lifecycle

Available → negotiated → accepted → prepared → active → transformed or disputed → completed, failed, abandoned, settled, or litigated.

## Authored quests

Authored arcs carry major characters, faction decisions, crew development, Andromeda evidence, and irreversible world changes. They should expose multiple systemic approaches rather than bespoke one-use mechanics whenever possible.

## Procedural contracts

Procedural work emerges from economy, faction goals, shortages, travel, law, and world events. Templates use canonical constraints and authored complications. Generated contracts must be validated for route availability, timing, cargo capacity, legality, and reward.

## Failure

Failure often continues the story. Late delivery, partial rescue, exposed smuggling, missing evidence, collateral damage, or broken promises may create debt, warrants, damaged trust, alternate employers, legal hearings, or recovery missions.

## Quest UI

Show issuer, source, objective, deadline in world time, expected travel time, known legal exposure, payment, collateral, evidence requirements, dependencies, and uncertainties. Hidden information may exist, but accepted terms cannot be misleading through poor interface design.

## Quality checklist

Every important quest needs a clear motive, at least two credible approaches, systemic consequences, understandable failure, and one later callback. Avoid errands that exist only to increase playtime.
