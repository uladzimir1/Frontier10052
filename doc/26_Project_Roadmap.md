# Frontier 10052 — Project Roadmap

## Principles

Build validated vertical slices, not 500 shallow stars. Protect the defining systems: delayed information, persistent ship, human crew, consequential factions, and costly travel.

## Phase 0 — Foundation

Canon consolidation, numbered design documentation, content schemas, .NET architecture, website and launcher foundation, save prototype, simulation clock, market prototype, route prototype, art/audio direction, and production tooling.

## Phase 1 — Vertical slice

A dense Sol route containing:

- Earth-orbit, Mars, Belt, and Pluto locations;
- one aging configurable ship;
- four crew members and relationship conflict;
- trade, information provenance, debt, maintenance, and repair;
- local flight, docking, gravity-boundary departure, and one warp route;
- inspection/piracy encounter with non-combat solutions;
- short authored arc with persistent consequences;
- stable save/load and accessible controls.

## Phase 2 — MVP

Expand Sol and nearby real stars; deepen economy, law, faction reputation, crew arcs, contracts, exploration, salvage, combat, procedural events, website accounts, and cloud-save readiness.

## Phase 3 — Early access

Increase content density, add regional arcs, improve tools, optimize performance, harden migrations, expand accessibility, establish telemetry and support, and validate progression to the Threshold.

## Phase 4 — Version 1.0

Deliver the coherent journey from Sol through frontier regions and the legal path into Andromeda, followed by meaningful stay/serve/expose/profit/escape outcomes. Polish presentation, audio, cinematics, onboarding, balance, stability, and localization.

## Post-launch

New systems, ships, crew, faction arcs, modding expansion, selected asynchronous social features, and multiplayer prototypes only after the single-player experience is proven.

## Stage gates

Each phase requires player comprehension, stable frame pacing, save durability, content throughput, accessibility, measurable fun without combat, manageable operating cost, and a realistic staffing plan.
