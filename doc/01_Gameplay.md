# Frontier 10052 — Gameplay

## Experience

Gameplay combines trading, route planning, delayed information, ship operations, crew relationships, law, exploration, narrative investigation, and dangerous real-time encounters. Systems must interact: a cargo decision affects mass, fuel, legality, insurance, crew acceptance, handling time, route options, and escape performance.

## Primary player verbs

Investigate, verify, plan, negotiate, buy, sell, borrow, insure, hire, assign, maintain, repair, fly, dock, evade, fight, rescue, salvage, testify, bribe, forge, conceal, and decide.

## Session structure

A normal session includes one or more runs between locations. The player gathers intelligence, chooses opportunities, prepares the ship, travels, resolves events, arrives with potentially stale knowledge, conducts business, and absorbs consequences.

## Progression

Progression is horizontal before vertical:

- more reliable systems and specialized modules;
- trusted contacts and better information sources;
- stronger crew cohesion and new expertise;
- legal privileges and restricted-market access;
- route knowledge and safer operating procedures;
- reputation, favors, evidence, and social leverage.

## Failure

Failure should usually continue play. Debt, damaged cargo, crew injury, missed deadlines, exposed contraband, surrender, arrest, and prison create new states and opportunities. Reloading must not be the only sensible response.

## MVP slice

The first playable slice should contain Sol locations, one configurable ship, four crew roles, a functioning trade market, information provenance, maintenance, local flight, one warp route, docking, one hostile encounter, and a short consequential story arc. It must remain satisfying without firing a weapon.
