# Frontier 10052 — Crew

## Roles

Initial roles are pilot, engineer, security, and medic. Later roles include navigator, broker, steward, scientist, gunner, systems specialist, and passenger liaison.

## Character model

Each crew member has identity, professional skills, traits, personal history, beliefs, faction sympathies, relationships, needs, boundaries, ambitions, injuries, stress, fatigue, morale, trust in the captain, and memories of player decisions.

Traits such as steady, pious, greedy, loyal, ambitious, fearful, secretive, compassionate, or vengeful must create contextual behavior rather than flat bonuses.

## Assignments

Crew are assigned to stations, watches, repairs, negotiations, boarding defense, medical response, shore leave, and special tasks. Expertise reduces risk but workload and fatigue matter.

## Relationships

Crew form friendships, rivalries, romances, debts, mentorships, suspicions, and ideological conflicts. Relationships affect cooperation, information sharing, stress, and willingness to remain aboard.

## Orders and refusal

Orders can be obeyed, questioned, delayed, sabotaged, or refused depending on loyalty, values, legality, fear, competence, and relationships. The player may persuade, threaten, compensate, replace, forgive, discipline, or accept refusal.

## Recruitment and departure

Recruitment depends on location, reputation, wages, ship condition, faction ties, and current events. Crew may leave, demand changes, betray the captain, retire, be arrested, die, or return later.

## MVP

Ship with four distinct crew members, role assignments, fatigue, morale, trust, one relationship conflict, one personal request, memory of a captain decision, and at least two non-combat ways crew expertise changes an outcome.
